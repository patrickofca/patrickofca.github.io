Frequently Asked Questions
How do I add a new box?
Tap the + button on the home screen and select New Box. Give your box a name and optional description, then tap Save. You can start adding items to it right away.
How does the AI scanning work?
Point your camera at any item and tap the scan button. PackSnap's AI will identify what it sees and suggest a label. You can confirm with one tap or edit the label before saving.
How do I generate and print a QR code for a box?
Open any box and tap the QR Code button in the top right. You can share the QR image directly to AirDrop, Messages, or your photo library, then print it from there. Stick it on your box — scanning it will instantly show everything inside.
Is my data backed up?
Yes. PackSnap automatically syncs your inventory to the cloud whenever you're connected to Wi-Fi or cellular. Your data is accessible from any device logged in to your account.
What's included in PackSnap Pro?
PackSnap Pro unlocks unlimited boxes, unlimited items, multi-item scanning, and unlimited AI scans. The free version includes a limited number of boxes and AI scans to get you started.
How do I restore a purchase?
Go to Settings → PackSnap Pro → Restore Purchase. Make sure you're signed in to the same Apple ID used when you originally purchased. If the issue persists, contact us and we'll sort it out.
I found a bug. How do I report it?
Email us at support@getpacksnap.com with a brief description of what happened and your device model (e.g., iPhone 15). Screenshots are always helpful. We take every bug report seriously.
Quick Tips
📸

Better scans

Good lighting makes a big difference. Lay items flat on a clean surface for the most accurate AI identification.

🏷️

Label your boxes first

Create and name all your boxes before scanning items. It's faster to assign items to existing boxes as you go.

🔍

Search anything

The search bar finds items across all boxes at once. Try searching by item type, color, or room.

📦

QR codes stick best

Print QR labels on sticker paper and place them on the short side of boxes — easy to spot when stacked.
